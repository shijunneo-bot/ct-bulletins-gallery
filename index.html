CleverTap Field Guide · No.14 · Bulletins

The campaign your business fires, not your user.

Every other surface waits for the user to act. Bulletins waits for your business to act, a new episode, a restock, a rate change, then fans that one moment out to the interest segment that cares, as Rich Push, Email, or SMS. Like a kitchen pass bell: one ring, and only the tables that ordered it turn.

The essence

What Bulletins is

One business event, raised via API with no user attached, fanned out to the interest segment that cares. Below is the whole idea in four cards.

What it is

The campaign your business fires, not your user. One business event, raised via API with no user identity, fans out to the interest segment that cares.

Trigger model

A backend POST to /1/targets/trigger.json carries business_event, name, properties (strings), c-by, when. CleverTap matches an event property to a user property to find recipients.

Three channels

The matched message goes out as Rich Push, Email, or SMS, per CleverTap's Bulletins product page. Set a push TTL and a control group so you can measure lift.

Design-control model

Dashboard owns the event, the interest segment, the message, control group, and TTL. Your backend owns the one trigger call that starts it.

Anatomy

What you set where

Gold is configured in the dashboard. Coral is code-side, in your backend. The clean split is the reason content teams can fire bulletins without touching the console.

Dashboard-configurable

  • The business event: name, properties, optional goal, and the user-property value it must match.
  • The interest segment (PBS or Custom List, in the legacy Segment Builder under Find People). Per the Segmentation Rules docs, include and exclude segments are not available for Bulletins.
  • The message and its channel: Rich Push, Email, or SMS.
  • The custom or campaign control group.
  • The push TTL.

Code-side (your backend)

  • The trigger call from your backend: POST /1/targets/trigger.json with business_event, name, properties, c-by, when.
  • Casting any numeric match key to a string on both sides (mapped values match as strings).
  • Raising the event automatically from a CMS, PIM, or catalogue webhook so teams never touch the dashboard.
  • The region endpoint (sg1 for Singapore); a wrong region fails silently.
Build vs buy

Three lanes for the fan-out

The spectrum is about how you orchestrate business-event fan-out, not how you render a surface. Start on Lane 1, grow into Lane 2, and only reach for Lane 3 if your matching is a real competitive asset.

Lane 1

Buy (native Bulletins)

Define the business event, publish the bulletin, raise it via trigger.json. You own the trigger. CleverTap owns matching, fan-out, control group, TTL, delivery, and stats.

Best when your interest logic fits the segment builder and you want speed.
Lane 2

Hybrid (recommended)

Native trigger and match, but pair the dispatch with Catalog STP, Linked Content, or Recommendations for a per-user body, and fire the event automatically from your CMS or catalogue so teams never open the dashboard.

Best for high release volume where the message should feel one-to-one. Pairing inside the body: verify with PM.
Lane 3

Build (roll your own)

On each business event, your backend queries your own interest data, then fires per-user External Triggers into a journey or hits campaign APIs per matched user.

Only when your matching is a genuine competitive asset. You re-own dedupe, caps, control groups, and error handling.
Pairing and triggers

How it sits in the stack

Ships across three channels

A Bulletin dispatches as Rich Push, Email, or SMS. The rich-push treatment (Field Guide 02) applies to what lands on the lock screen.

Pairs with Catalog and Linked Content

Fire the bulletin on the business event, then let Catalog STP or Linked Content render the exact item and live price in the body. Confirm body support with PM.

Sits beside External Trigger

Same backend-API shape, opposite target. Bulletins fans out with no user in the payload; External Trigger names one user.

Sits beside Reminders

Both are proactive. Reminders counts down to one user's deadline and self-cancels; Bulletins broadcasts one business moment.

Triggered from your systems

A CMS publish, a PIM restock, or a pricing job can call trigger.json, so content and merchandising fire bulletins without the dashboard.

Not a Journey node (as documented)

No documented Journey entry off a business event. If a client needs multi-step orchestration, flag it as an open item with PM.

Measurement

What you can measure, and what to confirm

What the dashboard reports

  • Percentage of Users Converted: the share of qualified users who were sent the bulletin.
  • Viewed and clicked counts on the same graph.
  • Total technical and non-technical delivery errors, with a technical-error breakdown table.
  • A Bulletin Activity Log: dispatched bulletins, event time, total sent, and who created each.

Revenue, funnel, control-group lift, and the exact attribution window are not documented for Bulletins specifically. Campaign-level governance (frequency caps, DND, engagement limits) is documented for campaigns generally and applies to Bulletins as a campaign type; confirm the Bulletin-specific behaviour.

Verify with your PM before you scope

  • Plan and packaging entitlement (the product-release page says Advanced; the features and pricing pages say Content Personalization add-on).
  • Whether Liquid Tags, Catalogs, Catalog STP, or Recommendations compile inside the Bulletin body, especially for email.
  • Whether a business event can serve as a Journey entry trigger.
  • Full analytics depth: revenue, funnel, control-group lift, and the exact conversion attribution window.
  • Interplay with global frequency caps, DND hours, and cross-campaign dedupe.
  • Whether NextGen Segment Builder support has shipped since the docs' planned note.
  • Whether RenderMax applies to Bulletin-dispatched push.
FAQ

Twenty straight answers

What is a Bulletin, in one sentence?›
An automated campaign that fires off a business event you raise via API and fans out to the interest segment that cares, instead of you building a campaign per SKU.
What is the difference between a business event and a user event?›
A user event is something the user does (watched, bought, viewed). A business event is something your business does (released, restocked, repriced). Bulletins trigger on the second and match against the first.
How is this different from Reminders?›
Reminders counts down to one user's deadline and cancels itself when they act. Bulletins fans one business moment out to a crowd. Deadline-per-user is Reminders; supply-event-to-many is Bulletins.
How is it different from External Trigger?›
External Trigger names a specific user in the payload and acts on that one person. A Bulletin's payload carries no user identity, so CleverTap decides who gets it from the interest segment. One-to-one versus one-to-many.
Why not just build a Journey with an event trigger?›
You often can. Bulletins earn their place when the trigger is a business event, it recurs at high volume, and you want to skip building a campaign each time.
Does the business event carry a user ID?›
No. The trigger call is business_event, name, properties, c-by, and when. Who receives it is resolved by the interest segment, not the payload.
How does the interest match work?›
You map a business event property (say genre) to a user event property, then add a count condition (watched this genre 10+ times recently). Users who satisfy the segment plus the match qualify.
Why only the string data type for mapped values?›
CleverTap supports only the string data type for the mapped property values. If your match key is numeric, cast it to a string on both sides. This is the most common setup gotcha.
Can I use the NextGen Segment Builder?›
Not for Bulletins as documented. Build and save your segment under Find People with the legacy builder, then select it. Whether NextGen has since shipped is worth confirming with your PM.
Which channels can a Bulletin dispatch through?›
Push, Email, and SMS, per CleverTap's Bulletins product page. The product docs walk through push in detail; email and SMS are named on the Bulletins and product-release pages. Channels beyond these three are not confirmed.
Can one business event drive more than one bulletin?›
You can publish more than one bulletin off the same business event, each with its own interest segment and channel. Confirm fan-out and dedupe behaviour with your PM for your setup.
How do I raise a business event?›
POST to /1/targets/trigger.json on your region's endpoint with the event name and properties. It is a backend call, so a CMS or catalogue system can fire it automatically.
What is the endpoint for my APAC accounts?›
Singapore is sg1.api.clevertap.com. Others: India in1, US us1, EU api.clevertap.com, Indonesia aps3, UAE mec1. Using the wrong region endpoint fails silently, so match it to the account's data region.
Is there a control group?›
Yes. You apply a custom or campaign control group during setup, so you can measure lift the same way you would on a normal campaign.
What is the TTL for?›
It sets how long the push stays valid to deliver. Size it to how time-sensitive the event is: a live fixture wants a short TTL; a new-season nudge can run longer.
How is performance measured?›
A Percentage of Users Converted graph (qualified users sent, plus viewed and clicked), technical and non-technical delivery errors, and an activity log of what dispatched, when, how many, and by whom.
What counts as a conversion?›
The optional Goal you set on the business event, such as app launched or movie watched. The exact attribution window is worth confirming with your PM.
Do Bulletins respect frequency caps, DND, and dedupe?›
Control group is confirmed. The interplay with global frequency caps, DND windows, and cross-campaign dedupe is worth nailing down with your PM before a client fires high-volume bulletins.
What plan or packaging is Bulletins on?›
The sources conflict: a product-release page places it on Advanced, while the features and pricing pages list it under a Content Personalization add-on. Confirm current packaging with your PM before scoping it.
When should I not use Bulletins?›
When the trigger is a user action (use a triggered campaign or journey), when it is a per-user deadline (use Reminders), or when it targets one named user (use External Trigger).
